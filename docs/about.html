About Me

Education

I will be completing my Bachelor’s degree in Environmental studies this March after 3 and half years at UCSB. I have taken many formative classes during my time learning about a range of topics from the physics, chemistry, and biology, to environmental justice and what building sustainable communities looks like in the present time. Professional Development

While attending UCSB I have been fortunate enough to participate in a number of internships that have shaped my experience and the type work that I am looking for in the future.

I have spent the last 3 years working in the LEAF lab participating in a number of field experiences that are shown in greater depth in the Field Experiences section.

I have also worked with The Nature Conservancy offering field trips on Dangermond Preserve to students across the Lompoc school district teaching about the local Oakwoodland ecosystem. Most recently I have been working with UCSB’s Cheadle Center for Biodiversity and Ecological Restoration (CCBER) to give education walks to preschool students at both on campus child care centers.
